What is the Paterna?

Tree of the paterna (fruit) benefits and properties focus on medicinal use, antioxidants and profitability of the fruit. The subject paterna benefits and properties is a taboo subject because not all people have eaten the fruit.

Properties of the guama or paterna (Fruit): The paterna is an edible fruit along with all its species that are known as peptone, pepeto and kujin. Some studies say that the ancestors who lived in Central America used the fruit to make intoxicating drinks. At present the benefits of eating guama or paterna is still a taboo and a little exotic, because there is little information about the benefits.

FACTS

  • The paterna fruit is presented as a green pod, when it is in overripe has a color between green and yellow, so growing guama can be a good project to develop.

  • The sign that the fruit is in a good moment to harvest it is when the pod has swollen seeds and they have turned from a dark green to a light green color with minimal yellow spots.

  • The paterna fruit is normally harvested in winters and can rarely be harvested in winter, although it is quite possible if it is irrigated

It is believed that the guama fruit may contain properties:

  • Disinflammatory.
  • Relieves diabetes.
  • Improves the circulatory system.

Fun fact!

The pulp of the paterna has many antioxidants together with its seeds, so it is composed of mostly water, calories, fiber, calcium, phosphorus, iron, magnesium and in minimal amounts thiamine and niacin.
